
Reference games: Asteroids + Xevious MECHANICS SPEC: Core Objective: Race your custom vehicle up a forced-scrolling volcanic tunnel, surviving extreme hazards and racking up a massive score through high-stakes drifting stunts. Player Controls: Up Arrow (Thrust), Left/Right Arrows (Rotate Craft), Spacebar (Plasma Cannon). Moment-to-Moment Loop: The camera forces a continuous upward vertical scroll. The player must use momentum-based rotational thrust to steer around jagged walls, dodging erupting magma and blasting falling rocks to maintain speed and survive. Win Condition: Survive three escalating stages to successfully burst out of the volcano caldera. Lose Condition: Suffer three collisions with walls, debris, or magma, which completely depletes your hull integrity. Scoring Rules: Earn 10 points per second of survival distance. Destroying debris grants 50 points. Triggering the Inferno Drift bonus by thrusting within 20 pixels of an active hazard awards 500 outrageous bonus points. Progression Phases: The early phase features wide, forgiving molten rivers and a slow scroll. The mid phase escalates to narrow, twisting tunnels with active geysers. The late phase features maximum scrolling speed and rapid collapsing ceilings. Mechanics: 1. Drift Boost: Thrusting perfectly perpendicular to your current momentum triggers a 1.5x speed burst. A strict 3-second cooldown prevents spamming. 2. Lava Geysers: Static environmental hazards that erupt cyclically (2 seconds active, 2 seconds dormant). Flying directly over an active eruption deals damage, but close-proximity drifting feeds your score multiplier. 3. Falling Debris: Telegraphed by expanding shadows on the ground. Players can blast them, fracturing the debris into exactly 3 smaller, harmless, bouncing pebbles. Spawn limit is strictly capped at 5 massive debris hazards per screen. Feasibility Note: This concept is highly feasible for a standard browser arcade engine. It relies entirely on standard 2D vector collisions and simple deterministic momentum physics without soft-body simulations. Active objects are heavily capped at 12 hazards and 20 projectiles maximum, guaranteeing smooth performance well below engine limits.
